                              chrisolah Mod Rob Currie • 4 years ago

                              Hi Rob!

                              I'm not familiar with fixed window spectograms or CQT, nor do I have any personal experience with recurrent neural networks, so I'd take this with a very significant grain of salt.

                              I'd generally recommend against a recurrent neural network unless you have a really good reason to think it's the right thing. RNNs are nasty to train because of a problem called the vanishing/exploding gradient problem. There are some cases where they make sense. For example, RNNs are often used if you have an output corresponding to every input.

                              Note that recurrent neural networks and convolutional neural networks aren't exclusive. You could convolve over the M dimension for each time slice, and then feed those into a recurrent architecture.

                              You'll probably want to experiment with things, but starting with convolutional networks probably make sense.
